# Problem Definition
## Boise Public Works Intelligence System (PWIS)

**Document Type:** Strategic Problem Framing
**Domain:** Water / Sewer / Stormwater Utility Infrastructure

---

## The Problem

Boise manages over 2,000 miles of underground utility pipe — 900+ miles of water distribution, 1,000+ miles of sanitary sewer, and hundreds of miles of stormwater collection. Much of this infrastructure was installed between 1940 and 1980 and is approaching or exceeding its expected service life.

Without a data-driven prioritization system, capital investment decisions for pipe replacement and rehabilitation are made based on emergency response patterns, political pressure, or institutional memory. This leads to:

1. **Reactive spending:** Emergency repairs cost 3–5x more than planned replacements
2. **Inequitable investment:** Districts with the loudest complaints get funding, not those with the worst infrastructure
3. **Regulatory risk:** Sanitary sewer overflows (SSOs) and water quality violations from deferred maintenance
4. **Budget inefficiency:** CIP dollars spent on low-priority assets while critical pipes deteriorate

## The Solution

PWIS provides a systematic, transparent, and auditable framework for utility infrastructure investment prioritization. It scores every pipe segment on six factors (condition, break history, capacity, criticality, material risk, age), produces tiered action recommendations, and runs scenario analyses to support budget planning.

## Key Stakeholders

- **Public Works Director** — Uses priority tiers and scenario analysis for CIP budget presentations to Council
- **Utility Supervisors** — Use district-level rankings to plan crew deployment and work orders
- **Finance Department** — Uses deferral cost analysis to justify multi-year CIP funding requests
- **City Council** — Reviews executive dashboard for policy-level decisions on system investment
- **Regulatory Compliance** — Uses condition and capacity data for EPA/DEQ reporting

## Success Metrics

- Reduction in emergency repair rate (target: 20% decrease in Year 1)
- Increase in planned vs. reactive maintenance ratio
- CIP budget utilization above 90% (vs. current pattern of underspending on planned work)
- All Critical-tier pipes addressed within 2 CIP cycles
- Equitable distribution: no district receives >40% of annual budget
